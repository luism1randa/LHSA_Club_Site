Start Your Journey in Pre-Health

As pre-health students you are expected to go above and beyond in demonstrating your abilities and interest in your field. Maintaining good grades, participating in scientific research, and being involved in your community are all excellent ways of demonstrating your passion and commitment.

However, as fellow pre-health students we know this journey can be daunting. Which is why we have drawn from our experiences, success, and mistakes in order to provide you with the best resources available on campus, locally in Tucson, and online.

Keep in mind that some of our board members have participated in many of these programs previously so feel free to contact us at lhsa.ua@gmail.com or contact one of our board members directly for more information and advice!

Recognized Research Programs

FRONTERA Summer Internship - Focusing Research on the Border Area/p>

Participants are matched with faculty mentors engaged in biomedical and public health research that has an impact on border communities. Participants travel to the city of Nogales, visit health care facilities and public health agencies on both sides of the US-Mexico border, and participate in service learning activities. Concurrently, participants develop an in-depth understanding of the pathway to medical school: including study and test-taking skills; preparations for the MCAT & GRE and drafting a personal statement. In the final week, participants present the outcome of their research on the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Consortium (UROC) poster session.

Volunteer

Give back your community, gain clinical hours, and more

Carondelet St. Mary's Hospital

If you're looking to gain hands-on experience in a hospital setting St. Mary's is a great place to start. They provide flexible schedules which is great if you're trying to juggle school and work as well. Volunteering is also a great way to meet healthcare professionals in many fields and gain direct access to shadowing opportunities. Applications are taken all year round. Fill out the application below and take it to the front desk at St. Mary's Hospital to begin your process.

A few things to keep in mind:

  • Requirements include

    • Agreeing to a minimum of 100 hours in a one-year period
    • providing two letters of reference
    • passing a criminal background check and drug screen

  • 75 hours are required at one of the following positions when beginning. After which you will be eligible to participate in places such as the Post Anesthesia Care Unit, Emergency Department, Intensive Care Unit in a more hands-on role that will allow you to have direct interaction with healthcare faculty and patients.

    • Information Desk
    • Intensive Care Unit
    • Surgery Lobby
    • Admissions Reception
    • Outpatient Rehabilitation

Feel free to visit the site or call 520-872-5747

Z-Mansion - The WORKship Project

It’s called The WORKship Project and every Sunday morning from 9:00 am until 11:30 am, this group of volunteers—mostly families—feeds between 100 to 300 homeless Tucsonans at the Z Mansion downtown. In addition to serving Sunday brunch, WORKship volunteers also simultaneously staff a clothing bank, help run an extensive first-aid station, entertain their guests, and whip up over 150 lunch bags for those in need.

Here's some of the stuff you might see if you help with the first-aid station:

  • Treated a homeless man for suspected gangrene and arranged for him to be seen that same day by a volunteer physician,
  • Treated another patient for two barbed wire lacerations and referred him to the doctors at El Rio Clinic for follow-up and a tetanus shot,
  • Examined a woman for a leg injury and determined and treated her for a probable case of Grade 1 semimembranosis tendonitis (a mild hamstring tear),
  • Examined a patient for a painful shin condition and then treated his probable medial tibial stress syndrome (shin splints) by taping his lower leg, and,
  • Examined a young woman who had recently been attacked, determined that she had probable fractures of her right fourth and fifth metacarpal bones (or "Boxer’s fractures" of her right hand) in addition to a possible scaphoid bone fracture, stabilized her arm and hand, and then referred her to immediate treatment at St. Mary’s Hospital.

The WORKship Project is held every Sunday morning of the year, starting at 9:00 am and ending at 11:30 am at the Z Mansion at 288 North Church Avenue, Tucson, Arizona 85701. Volunteers of all ages—including families—need not call ahead, but are invited to come down to help out any Sunday throughout the year.

Pre-MD Abassador Program

The PreMD Ambassadors program is a unique opportunity that gives undergraduate students interested in medicine, access to incredible resources at the College of Medicine – Tucson. PreMD Ambassadors work with the Admissions Office on a variety of projects, assist with major events, participate in community service and lead tours, while learning indepth skills to prepare them for their own medical school application

As a PreMD Ambassador, you will learn about the educational paths that you and your fellow students can take to medical school, and about the programs and opportunities offered by the College of Medicine. The program lasts for a full academic year, during which you'll remain enrolled full-time in your undergraduate major. Each ambassador functions with little supervision. They maintain a high degree of professionalism, reliability, and confidentiality.
